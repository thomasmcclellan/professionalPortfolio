Profile

A little about me.

Tom McClellan


I first realized my passion for development when I was applying to work as a specialist with SalesForce. My friend encouraged me to teach myself SQL with Codecademy to help my application process. While I was working through SQL, I realized that it was enjoyable, so I kept the studies going through HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more. With each step further, I realized that I enjoyed this enough to pursue it as a vocation.

I have realized that coding is a compilation of two things: language learning and problem solving. When I was earning my Masters degree, I needed to learn Hebrew and Greek. While learning these two languages, I realized how to pick up different languages; learning code is no different. Furthermore, I have always enjoyed puzzles and, while working through my code, I feel as though I am working my ways through an elaborate puzzle (which is fun for me).

I have been attending ElevenFifty since mid-August. And, over the course of these twelve weeks, I expect to learn how to proficiently develop. Furthermore, I am learning how to find answers to questions I am stumped on. This skill will help me progress as I continue in my IT career.

Professional Philosophy

Here are some qualities that I will bring to your workplace.

Personal Accountability

Where it is easy to get anything off of the internet and claim it to be yours, I am invested in holding myself to a standard of creating out of my own efforts.

Goal Achievement

I am heavily invested in completing whatever I am working on. I will reach my goals with punctuality and quality.

Problem Solving

As is common with coding, I will come across problems that I struggle with. I will work through these problems until the answer comes.

Conceptual Thinking

It is not enough to know basic formulae or algorithms; while learning how to code, I will continue to develop skills for me to see the bigger picture and not get lost in the trees.

Self-Management

I will hold myself to keeping any and all deadlines. I am not an employee that requires handholding or micromanagement; rather, I personally invested in getting my work done when it needs to be.

Resiliency

Where it is easy to get bogged down by the difficulties of coding, I can endure through the struggles in order to find the answer to the problems.

Teamwork

Though I can work alone, I am also a very good team player. I am more focused on the needs of the team rather than my own.
